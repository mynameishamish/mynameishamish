Micbot Group Robotics Research

Research and Design Lead

In 2018 I was working in a research lab focused on studying the effects of a robot on interpersonal group dynamics, which is a very young field. Robots in this field are mostly used in a participant role, often anthropomorphic, they are placed as an equal part of the group with humans. I pushed to design and study a robot in a new paradigm, whereby the robot was there as a ‘peripheral robotic object'. This would position the robot as an extension of the environment meant to benefit but not be a part of the group. This new idea formed into its own research project that I lead and would become the lead author in publishing.

Research results. Our robot and its pro social behaviours caused the group to collaborate better and perform better on a problem solving task.
Project Information

After a number of experiments and a full research study over 18 months, we published results showing positive group outcomes in a top conference in the field. The groups of participants in our study scored higher on collaborative tasks when the robot acted with the behaviours I created. To date 65+ other academic papers have cited our paper since 2019, among the top cited papers from the entire conference that year.
